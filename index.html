ABOUT ME

Welcome, I am Xian Wu

I am a UX researcher who is enthusiastic about bridging the gaps between human and technology via user-centric-development. I obtained my Ph.D. degree in Computer Science and Engineering from University of South Carolina. Advised by Dr. Jenay M. Beer — an engineering psychologist — I was trained in a diverse interdisciplinary environment consisting of computer scientists, social scientists, neuropsychologists, and designers. My research focus was — and is — Human Computer/Robot Interaction. I have published in many different areas, including human factors & ergonomics, gerontechnology, HRI, and public health. I am experienced in developing and deploying research plans, from identifying research objectives and conducting literature reviews to implementing qualitative & quantitative methodologies (including, but not limited to structured/semi-structured interviews, persona building, journey mapping, heuristic evaluation, usability testing, focus group, and questionnaire & surveys development) and performing data analysis. With over 8+ years of combined experience as a user experience researcher and designer, I am passionate about investigating the use of technology to improve peoples’ lives. I also enjoy public speaking and teaching. I was a lecturer for HTML and JavaScript for over 400 students while I attended grad school. I have been actively engaged in advocating women in STEM, and have presented in professional conferences (e.g., Grace Hopper), colleges, middle schools, and communities where I voice the importance of STEM education. In my spare time, I love hanging out with my 4 adopted fur friends – 3 cats and a dog. I am also an amateur florist as well as garden rose fanatic.
